# Conference Management System - User Guide for Non-Technical Users

## Welcome to Our Conference Platform! 🎓

This guide explains how our website works in simple terms, perfect for explaining to conference organizers, speakers, and attendees.

---

## 🌟 How It Works: The Complete Story

### **Chapter 1: Before the Conference Begins**

**Step 1: Conference Creation (Admin Only)**
- University administrators create a new conference event
- They set dates, location, themes/topics, and important deadlines
- They activate the conference when everything is ready
- *Think of this as "opening the doors" to your virtual conference hall*

**Step 2: Adding Key Information**
- Admins add keynote speakers (famous professors or industry experts)
- They create the conference schedule with sessions and time slots
- Committee members are listed (organizing team, scientific committee)
- *This is like setting up the stage and program before guests arrive*

---

### **Chapter 2: Researchers Submit Their Work**

**For Authors Who Want to Present:**

Anyone can submit a research paper without creating an account first! Here's how:

1. Visit the conference page
2. Click "Submit Paper"
3. Fill in:
   - Paper title and abstract (summary)
   - All authors' names and emails
   - Institution and country
   - Choose a theme/topic
4. Submit and receive a tracking ID (like SUB-2025-0001)

**What happens next?**
- The submission goes to "PENDING" status
- Reviewers (committee members) read and evaluate the paper
- Status changes to: ACCEPTED, REJECTED, or needs revision
- Authors can check their status anytime using their tracking ID

---

### **Chapter 3: Creating Your Account (Participants)**

**NOW ANYONE CAN CREATE AN ACCOUNT!** 🎉

You don't need to submit a paper to join. There are two types of participants:

#### **Type A: Attendees (Listeners)**
- People who want to attend and listen to presentations
- Students, researchers, or anyone interested in the topic
- They don't present but participate in Q&A and networking

#### **Type B: Presenting Authors**
- Researchers whose papers were accepted
- They both attend AND present their work

**How to Register:**

**Option 1: Create Account Without Conference**
- Just provide your name, email, affiliation, country
- You now have a permanent profile
- Later you can register for any conference

**Option 2: Register for a Specific Conference**
- Provide your details + choose a conference
- You get both an account AND conference registration
- Receive a registration ID (like REG-2025-0001)

**Key Point:** Everyone can create an account anytime, even if no conference is currently active!

---

### **Chapter 4: The Conference Experience**

**For Attendees:**
- Browse the full schedule of sessions
- See all speakers and their presentation topics
- View accepted papers and abstracts
- Join sessions at scheduled times
- Ask questions during Q&A

**For Speakers:**
- Everything attendees can do, PLUS:
- Access to speaker preparation materials
- Upload presentation slides
- Receive speaker certificate after presenting

**Special Feature: Reports**
After attending sessions, participants can write reports:
- Share what they learned
- Provide feedback on sessions
- Suggest improvements for future conferences
- Admins review and approve reports

---

### **Chapter 5: After the Conference**

**Certificates Are Generated Automatically:**
- **Participant Certificate**: For everyone who attended
- **Speaker Certificate**: Special certificate for presenters
- Each certificate has a unique verification code
- Anyone can verify certificates online

**Reports Get Published:**
- Approved reports become part of conference records
- Help improve future events
- Show community engagement

---

## 👥 Who Does What?

### **Administrators (University Staff)**
- Create and manage conferences
- Add/edit speakers (speakers don't need accounts)
- Review paper submissions
- Accept/reject papers
- Manage registrations
- Generate certificates
- Approve participant reports
- Update committee member lists

### **Speakers (Invited by University)**
- Don't need to create accounts
- Added by admins with their bio and presentation info
- Visible to all participants
- Receive speaker certificates

### **Authors/Researchers**
- Submit papers (no account needed initially)
- Track submission status
- If accepted: register as participant+speaker
- Present their research
- Receive certificates

### **Attendees/Participants**
- Create free accounts anytime
- Register for conferences
- Attend sessions and listen
- View all conference information
- Write reports about their experience
- Receive participation certificates

### **Committee Members**
- Listed on the conference page
- Can be reviewers or editors
- Help evaluate submissions
- Managed by administrators

## ❓ Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: Do I need an account to submit a paper?**
A: No! Submit first, create account later if your paper is accepted.

**Q: Can I create an account if no conference is happening?**
A: Yes! Create your profile anytime, then register when conferences open.

**Q: What's the difference between a speaker and a presenter?**
A: Speakers are invited guests (keynotes). Presenters are authors with accepted papers. Both present, but speakers are added by admins while presenters apply through submission.

**Q: Do speakers need accounts?**
A: No! The university manages speaker information. Speakers don't need to log in.

**Q: What if I just want to learn, not present?**
A: Perfect! Create an account as "ATTENDEE" and register for conferences. You'll get full access to sessions and a participation certificate.

**Q: How do I know my paper was accepted?**
A: Use your tracking ID to check status online, or wait for email notification.

**Q: What are reports?**
A: Short articles participants write about what they learned. Think of them as conference reflections or feedback.

**Q: Can one person have multiple roles?**
A: Yes! You could be an attendee for one conference, a presenter for another, and write reports for both.
